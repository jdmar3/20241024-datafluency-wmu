Data Futures

Literacy and Fluency in a New Era of Information Overload

John D Martin III, PhD

Introduction

1. Begin with a series of questions to engage the audience:
   - "Who here interacts with data as part of your work?"
   - "How many people here today have interacted with a database today?"
   - "How many of you could give a definition of what a database is?"
2. Purpose of the Talk: Highlight the importance of data literacy and fluency in an era of information overload.
3. Key Definitions: Clarify the difference between data literacy and data fluency.

The Current State of Data Production and Consumption

1. Data Explosion: Discuss the rapid growth in data production.
2. Impact on Research Institutions and Higher Education: Challenges in making informed decisions.

Data Literacy: A Critical Foundation

1. Definition and Scope: Ability to read, interpret, and critically evaluate data.
2. Current Gaps: Gaps in education and professional development.
3. Real-World Examples: Negative outcomes due to lack of data literacy.

Moving from Data Literacy to Data Fluency

1. Defining Data Fluency: Capacity to communicate insights and apply data in decision-making.
2. Why Fluency Matters: Enables innovation and informed, data-driven decisions.

Challenges and Opportunities

1. Challenges: Educational inertia, resource gaps, and overwhelming pace of data production.
2. Opportunities: Adaptation and leadership in building data skills.

Strategies for Building Data Capacity

1. Educational Strategies: Ways for schools, universities, and training programs to foster skills.
2. Organizational Approaches: Integrating data training into workplace culture.
3. Individual Action: Encouraging personal development of data skills.

Conclusion

1. Key Takeaways: Importance of moving from data literacy to fluency.
2. Call to Action: Reflect on data capabilities and enhance skills.
